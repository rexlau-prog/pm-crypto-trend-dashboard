01 What this bot is

Polymarket lists recurring 5-minute "Up or Down" markets for crypto (BTC, ETH, SOL, BNB, HYPE…). Every 300 seconds a fresh market opens with a slug like btc-updown-5m-<unix_window>, trades two outcome tokens (YES = "up", NO = "down"), and resolves to exactly one side paying $1.00 at expiry. This bot connects to Polymarket's CLOB, streams the order book, and runs three independent strategies (each toggled by a config flag) that place real limit orders on those 5-minute markets. P&L is tracked per symbol, persisted to disk, and pushed to Telegram.

02 The three strategies

All three loops run concurrently inside one event loop and share the same order/fill plumbing. In the forked default config only Strong-Side is enabled; the other two are off.

⚡ Strong-Side Dip-BuyENABLED

Read the market's first 90 s, then buy the favoured side only if it's cheap, and hold to expiry.
  1. At rollover, record every YES/NO price tick for the first 90 s.
  2. Average each side over the window → the higher average is the strong side.
  3. Place a limit BUY on the strong side at min(0.50, best_ask) — only enters on a dip.
  4. Hold to expiry; win if the strong side's price > the other at settlement.
P&L/fill = (1.0−entry) if win else (−entry) × shares

⚖️ Dual-LimitDISABLED

Bid both YES and NO cheap. If both fill, you own a guaranteed-$1 pair for <$1 — a volatility arb.
  1. At rollover, place limit BUY on both YES and NO at 0.25 for each symbol.
  2. Monitor fills via REST for up to 270 s.
  3. Cancel whatever is still unfilled at the end of the window.
  4. Book P&L by fill outcome; persist to dual_pnl.json.

06 Setup blockers, risks & gotchas

Findings from the code review — read these before running real money through it.

BLOCKER Missing dependency: py_clob_client_v2

Every order goes through py_clob_client_v2, but it is not on PyPI, not vendored in the repo, and not in requirements.txt. It's a customised fork of Polymarket's py-clob-client. You must obtain/build it (ask your partner for the package) before the bot will even import. eth_account and httpx are also imported but missing from requirements.

SECURITY No .gitignore → private-key leak risk

The bot reads your Polygon private key from .env, but the repo shipped with no .gitignore. One stray git add . would commit your key. A .gitignore and .env.example have been added in this fork — keep them.

CORRECTNESS Dual-limit one-sided P&L is pessimistic

When only one leg fills, P&L is booked as −price×shares regardless of how that side actually resolves. A one-sided fill that wins ($1) is not credited — real P&L can be better than the log shows.

OPS Self-restart needs an external supervisor

At the end of the trading window the bot calls os._exit(0) to reload code. Without systemd / pm2 / a restart loop, it just dies and won't trade the next session.

HYGIENE Stale config.example.py

The example config predates the .env refactor and the dual/strong-side params — it even claims config.py is gitignored (it isn't). Treat config.py + the new .env.example as the source of truth.

DESIGN Latency & fee assumptions

Edges here are thin (cents per share). Real fills depend on Polymarket queue priority, the 3% sell haircut, and order latency. The committed P&L is tiny-stake validation, not proof of scalable edge. Backtest/paper-trade before sizing up.
